MARK 12 — QUESTIONS IN THE TEMPLE
MARK 13
ESV STUDY BIBLE • MARK 12:1–44

Questions in
the Temple

Christ's Authority and the Heart of True Devotion

CORE THEME
Christ's authority. Fruitfulness. Wholehearted love. True devotion over hypocrisy.
SUMMARY

In the Temple courts during the final days before the cross, Jesus tells the Parable of the Tenants exposing the leaders' rejection of the beloved Son, masterfully answers traps about taxes and the resurrection, declares the two greatest commandments, reveals Himself as David's Lord, and holds up a poor widow's sacrificial giving as the model of true devotion that shames religious hypocrisy.

CONTEXT
Year: c. A.D. 30 (Tuesday of Passion Week)
Location: The Temple courts in Jerusalem (Judea)
People: Jesus, the chief priests, scribes, Pharisees, Herodians, Sadducees, a sincere scribe, and a poor widow
In Jesus’ Life: The last week before the crucifixion; a day of intense public confrontation and authoritative teaching that seals the religious leaders' determination to kill Him.
SIX MOVEMENTS
1
THE TENANTS
12:1–12

The Parable of the Tenants

Jesus tells the story of a vineyard owner who sends servants — and finally his beloved son — to receive fruit from wicked tenants who beat, shame, and kill them.

2
TAXES TO CAESAR
12:13–17

Paying Taxes to Caesar

The Pharisees and Herodians try to trap Jesus with a question about paying taxes to Caesar. Jesus exposes their hypocrisy and delivers a profound answer about allegiance.

3
THE RESURRECTION
12:18–27

The Sadducees and the Resurrection

The Sadducees present a hypothetical scenario of seven brothers and one widow to mock the idea of resurrection. Jesus corrects both their understanding of Scripture and the power of God.

4
THE GREAT COMMANDMENT
12:28–34

The Great Commandment

A sincere scribe asks which commandment is most important. Jesus answers with the Shema and the command to love one’s neighbor, and declares the scribe not far from the kingdom of God.

5
WHOSE SON?
12:35–37

Whose Son Is the Christ?

Jesus challenges the popular view that the Messiah is merely the son of David by pointing to Psalm 110, where David calls the Messiah “Lord.” The crowd listens with delight.

6
TRUE DEVOTION
12:38–44

Beware of the Scribes & The Widow’s Offering

Jesus warns against the pride and exploitation of the scribes, then watches a poor widow give two small coins — and declares that she has given more than all the rich.
